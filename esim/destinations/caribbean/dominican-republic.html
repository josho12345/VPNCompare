// Network operators

Dominican Republic Networks & Coverage

Claro

The largest network by a clear margin, covering 98% of tourist zones and offering the strongest rural and peninsula reach of any Dominican carrier. Most travel eSIM providers route through Claro for this reason.

Altice

The strong second option, covering 95% of tourist zones with excellent performance in Santo Domingo, Santiago, and Punta Cana. Slightly weaker than Claro once you head into rural areas.

// Coverage by area

Coverage Quality in the Dominican Republic

Punta Cana, Santo Domingo & Santiago

Coverage is excellent across every major tourist zone — Punta Cana, Bávaro, the Colonial Zone in Santo Domingo, Santiago, Puerto Plata, and Samaná all get strong 4G, with 5G now live in the three biggest cities.

The Bahoruco mountains & Haiti border

Away from the tourist corridor, coverage from all three networks drops to roughly 60–70% in mountainous areas like the Sierra de Bahoruco and near the Haitian border. Still generally usable, just less consistent than the coast.

💡 The one real exception: Isla Saona

Despite being one of the most popular day-trip excursions from Punta Cana and Bayahibe, Isla Saona is a protected nature reserve with no cell towers and no signal from any operator. It is not a coverage gap you can work around — genuinely plan to be offline for the day.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

A dedicated tourist police hotline

Beyond the standard emergency line, CESTUR (tourist police) has its own number: 809-200-3500, for theft, scams, or tour operator disputes. Worth saving separately from 911 for non-emergency issues.

One number for everything

The Dominican Republic runs a nationwide unified 911 system, expanded since 2014 to cover police, fire, and ambulance from a single call, free from any phone including mobiles with no active plan. Multilingual operators handle English and Spanish.

Airport SIM registration takes 30–60 minutes

Buying a physical Claro or Altice SIM at Punta Cana or Santo Domingo airport means passport ID and a real time cost — often half an hour or more after a long flight. eSIM tourist plans skip this entirely.
