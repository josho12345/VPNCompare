// What to expect

Coverage Across the USA

🏛

Cities and main corridors

New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Miami, Las Vegas, San Francisco, Seattle, and all major US cities have outstanding 4G/5G coverage. The main interstate highway network is well covered. Airports have excellent signal throughout.

🏔

National parks and rural interior

The Grand Canyon, Yellowstone, Yosemite, the Appalachian Trail, and large parts of rural Montana, Wyoming, Idaho, and the Dakotas have very limited or no coverage. The USA is the fourth largest country on Earth and much of its interior is genuinely remote.

💡 Driving through rural America

Long road trips on Route 66, through the Great Plains, or across the Desert Southwest will encounter genuine dead zones. Download offline Google Maps for your entire route before leaving any city. Save your accommodation addresses and emergency contacts offline before heading into remote areas.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 911

The USA uses 911 for all emergencies. Works from any phone without a plan. English-speaking operators throughout. In remote national park areas, call 911 and ask for park rangers specifically — they coordinate wilderness rescue.

🚌

Uber, Lyft, and domestic flights

Uber and Lyft operate in all major US cities. Your eSIM data is essential from the moment you clear customs — booking a ride, checking domestic flight connections, and navigating unfamiliar airports all require active data.

📱

Download offline maps for every state you visit

Google Maps offline downloads are state-sized in the USA. Download offline maps for every state on your itinerary before you travel — the files are large, do it on WiFi. Essential for national park driving routes where signal disappears.
