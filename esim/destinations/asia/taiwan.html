// Network operators

Taiwan Networks & Coverage

📡

Chunghwa Telecom

Taiwan's largest and oldest carrier. Best coverage across the island including remote east coast and mountain areas. 5G live in Taipei and major cities. eSIM supported. The benchmark carrier for reliable nationwide coverage in Taiwan.

📢

Taiwan Mobile

Second-largest carrier. Excellent coverage in the north and urban areas, competitive 5G rollout in Taipei and Taichung. eSIM supported on tourist plans. A solid alternative with good city performance and competitive pricing on international plans.

// What makes Taiwan different

Coverage Across Taiwan

⛺

Mountains and gorges

Taroko Gorge and the Central Mountain Range are a genuine test for any network — and Taiwan largely passes it. Coverage holds up through much of Taroko and along the east coast highway, though deep in certain gorge sections there are gaps. Better than most countries manage in equivalent terrain.

🏷

The night market advantage

Taiwan's famous night markets — Shilin, Raohe, Fengjia — are dense with people and vendors, which can stress any network. In practice 4G holds up fine in all major night market areas. The Taiwan High Speed Rail between cities gives consistent in-carriage signal throughout the journey.

💡 The mainland China contrast

Taiwan is geographically close to China but completely different in terms of internet access. Every app and service that is blocked on the Chinese mainland works without restriction in Taiwan. If you are crossing between the two on the same trip, your eSIM situation and online experience will be completely different on each side.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 110 / 119

Police is 110, fire and ambulance is 119. Both are free from any phone without a plan active. Taiwan has reliable English-speaking operators at major emergency centres. Standard of emergency response is high across the island.

🚌

EasyCard for transit

Taiwan's EasyCard is a rechargeable transit card used on MRT, buses, and convenience store purchases. This is separate from your eSIM — your data handles apps and navigation while EasyCard handles fares. Buy one at any MRT station on arrival.

📱

7-Eleven is everywhere

Taiwan has one of the world's highest 7-Eleven densities. They offer printing, bill payment, ticketing, and WiFi. Your eSIM data makes it easy to find the nearest one and use their services without the WiFi. Genuinely useful as a backup connectivity and services hub.

Protect your identity abroad

Taiwan's internet is open and unrestricted, but public WiFi networks carry the same risks as anywhere else. Avoid banking on public hotspots and use your eSIM data for sensitive logins. A VPN on public WiFi is good practice even in a free-internet country.
