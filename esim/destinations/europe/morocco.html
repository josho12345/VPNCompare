// Network operators

Morocco Networks & Coverage

📡

Maroc Telecom (IAM)

Morocco's largest carrier with the broadest coverage including in rural areas, the High Atlas, and desert routes. Solid 4G in Marrakech, Casablanca, Fes, and Rabat. eSIM supported. The recommended choice for any travel beyond the main cities.

📢

Orange Maroc

Strong competitor with excellent urban coverage in the main Moroccan cities. Good 4G in Marrakech and Casablanca. eSIM supported. Coverage drops off more quickly than Maroc Telecom outside major urban areas.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Morocco

🏛

Cities and tourist areas

Marrakech, Casablanca, Rabat, Fes, Agadir, Chefchaouen, and Essaouira all have reliable 4G coverage. The main tourist routes between these cities have reasonable signal. The coastal route from Casablanca to Agadir is well covered.

🏗

Desert, mountains, and remote areas

The Sahara Desert edges (Merzouga, M'Hamid, Zagora), High Atlas mountain passes, and remote Berber village routes have very limited or no coverage. Kasbah areas deep in the Draa Valley can be entirely without signal.

💡 Desert and mountain travel

Download offline Google Maps for your entire Moroccan route before leaving Marrakech. The route from Ouarzazate to Merzouga and all tracks into the Sahara should be treated as offline journeys. Save your riad or camp coordinates before leaving any town.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 190 / 15 / 150

Morocco's emergency numbers: police 190, ambulance 15, fire 150. Emergency calls work without a plan. In remote High Atlas and desert areas, response times are significantly longer than in cities.

🚌

Supratours buses and grand taxis

Supratours buses connect Marrakech with Agadir, Essaouira, and other towns. Grand taxis (shared long-distance taxis) cover routes between cities. InDriver and Careem work in Casablanca and Marrakech for app-based rides.

💲

Bargaining and medina navigation

Marrakech and Fes medinas are deliberately disorienting. Download offline Google Maps tiles for both medinas before entering — live signal can be poor in dense souk areas. Have local dirhams (MAD) — most medina vendors are cash only.
