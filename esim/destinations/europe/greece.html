// Network operators

Greece Networks & Coverage

📡

Cosmote

Greece's largest carrier with the broadest island and rural coverage. Excellent 4G in Athens, Thessaloniki, and all major tourist islands. eSIM supported. The recommended choice for travellers heading to less-visited islands.

📢

Vodafone Greece

Strong competitor with excellent coverage on the main tourist islands and in Athens. Good 5G in urban areas. eSIM supported. A solid alternative for travellers sticking to Santorini, Mykonos, Crete, and Rhodes.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Greece

🏛

Athens and main tourist islands

Athens, Thessaloniki, Santorini, Mykonos, Crete main towns, Rhodes, Corfu, and Zakynthos all have reliable 4G coverage. The Athens Metro has coverage throughout. Major ferry ports are generally well covered.

🌊

Remote islands and mainland mountains

Greece's 227 inhabited islands include many with very limited or no coverage away from their main settlements. Remote Aegean islands, parts of the Peloponnese mountains, and inland Epirus can have significant gaps.

💡 Island hopping and remote islands

If your trip involves island hopping beyond the main Cyclades and Ionian circuits, research coverage for your specific islands before travelling. Download offline maps, ferry schedules, and emergency contacts before leaving the mainland or a major hub island.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 112 or 100/199/166

Greece uses 112 as the EU emergency number, plus 100 (police), 199 (fire), and 166 (ambulance). All work without a plan. On remote islands, response times can be significantly longer.

⛴

Ferry schedules and booking

Greek island ferries are operated by multiple companies and schedules change seasonally. Use the Ferryhopper or OpenSeas apps to search and book — both work with your eSIM data. Download your tickets offline before boarding.

☁

Weather and wind alerts

The Aegean's Meltemi winds can cancel ferries with little notice during July and August. Keep your eSIM active and check Windy or the Poseidon weather app before any island-hopping day.
