🔒 Privacy & Security

Surfshark uses AES-256-GCM encryption on OpenVPN and IKEv2, and ChaCha20 on WireGuard — both industry standard. The kill switch performed reliably in all our tests, cutting traffic immediately on VPN drop rather than after a delay.

The no-log policy has been independently audited, which is a meaningful commitment. Our DNS and WebRTC leak tests returned clean results across all servers we tested. We found no IP leaks during our 30-day test period.

The one area where privacy purists will pause is jurisdiction. Surfshark is incorporated in the Netherlands, which sits within the 9 Eyes intelligence alliance. This means in theory a court order could compel Surfshark to log and hand over user data going forward — though there is no public record of this having happened and the audit found no logging infrastructure.
