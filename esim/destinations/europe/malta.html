// Network operators

Malta Networks & Coverage

GO

Malta's incumbent and largest operator, with roughly 50% market share and over 400,000 subscribers. Its 4G/5G network reaches over 99% of the population indoors and outdoors across both Malta and Gozo. eSIM activation is available directly through GO's app. The safest all-round choice for visitors.

Epic

Formerly Vodafone Malta, rebranded in 2021 after acquisition by Monaco Telecom. Named Malta's fastest mobile network by Ookla across multiple recent quarters, and operates a SIM kiosk in the airport arrivals hall. eSIM supported on tourist plans. The most visitor-friendly option, with instant airport setup.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Malta

Malta & Gozo, wall to wall

Valletta, Sliema, St Julian's, Mdina, and every town on Gozo have dependable 4G, with 5G covering effectively the entire population, among the highest rates in the EU. There's no real rural gap on an island this size.

Old stone buildings

Malta's defining feature is also its one honest signal complication: the thick limestone walls of Valletta's bastions, Mdina's fortifications, and older townhouses can weaken indoor signal and WiFi, even where outdoor coverage is excellent. All three networks now offer WiFi-calling to work around it.

🏰 The honest gap

Malta doesn't have a rural coverage story the way most destinations on this site do, it's simply too small, and all three networks report population coverage above 95%. The one genuine catch is architectural: the historic limestone construction found across Valletta, Mdina, and much of the older housing stock can noticeably weaken indoor signal, regardless of how strong coverage is on the street outside.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Emergency number: 112

Malta uses the EU standard: 112 covers police, fire, and ambulance alike, free from any phone, including those without an active SIM. Operators are fluent in Maltese and English, and can dispatch the Armed Forces of Malta's maritime rescue squadron for emergencies at sea.

Malta's railway closed in 1931

There are no trains here anymore — getting to Gozo means the Gozo Channel car ferry, running every 45 minutes between Ċirkewwa and Mġarr. The crossing takes about 25 minutes, and coverage holds up well the whole way across.

Bolt, Uber, and widespread WiFi

Both Bolt and Uber operate across Malta, alongside metered taxis. Cafes and public spaces commonly offer free WiFi — but always use your eSIM data for sensitive logins rather than public networks.
