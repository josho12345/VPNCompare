// Network operators

Switzerland Networks & Coverage

📡

Swisscom

Switzerland's largest carrier and the benchmark for Alpine coverage. Reaches ski resorts, mountain railways, and remote Alpine areas that most European networks cannot match. Excellent 4G/5G in Zurich, Geneva, Bern, and Basel. eSIM fully supported.

📢

Sunrise

Strong competitor with excellent urban and suburban coverage across all Swiss cities and major towns. Good 5G deployment in Zurich and Geneva. eSIM supported. Mountain coverage falls behind Swisscom significantly in remote areas.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Switzerland

⛺

Cities, resorts, and mountain railways

Zurich, Geneva, Bern, Basel, Lucerne, Interlaken, Zermatt, and St. Moritz all have excellent coverage. The Swiss rail network (SBB) has consistent signal on virtually all routes including mountain rack railways. Ski resort areas are well covered.

🗻

Remote Alpine valleys and passes

Even Switzerland has coverage gaps in the most remote Alpine terrain — deep valley floors, certain high passes, and backcountry hiking routes beyond resort areas. These gaps are rarer here than in any other mountain country in Europe, but they exist.

💡 Switzerland is not in the EU — data roaming rules differ

Switzerland is not an EU member state, which means EU roaming regulations do not automatically apply. Some European eSIM plans may charge differently or have separate Switzerland add-ons. Always verify your specific plan covers Switzerland before purchasing.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 112 or 117/118/144

Switzerland uses the EU-compatible 112, plus 117 (police), 118 (fire), and 144 (ambulance). Mountain rescue is 1414 (Rega helicopter rescue) — save this number if hiking in Alpine terrain.

🚌

SBB Swiss Rail — buy your pass in advance

The Swiss Travel Pass gives unlimited travel on trains, buses, and many mountain railways. Buy it before you arrive — it is significantly cheaper through the SBB website. The SBB Mobile app handles tickets and live delay information.

💳

Switzerland is expensive — plan your payments

Switzerland uses the Swiss Franc (CHF), not the Euro. It is one of the world's most expensive countries. Most tourist areas accept cards and mobile payment, but cash is useful in rural mountain villages.
