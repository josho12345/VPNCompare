// What to expect

Coverage Across Spain

🏛

Cities, coasts, and popular resorts

Madrid, Barcelona, Seville, Valencia, Malaga, Bilbao, and all main coastal resorts have excellent 4G/5G coverage. Major tourist islands — Majorca, Ibiza, Lanzarote, Tenerife main towns — are well covered. The AVE high-speed rail network has consistent signal.

🌎

Rural interior and remote areas

The Spanish meseta, rural Extremadura, parts of Castile-La Mancha, and the interior of Galicia can have intermittent coverage. Spain has the largest land area in Western Europe and genuinely rural areas to match.

💡 Driving through rural Spain

The road network through central Spain can have stretches of limited coverage. Download offline maps before driving through Extremadura, rural Aragon, or the interior of Galicia. Most Camino de Santiago routes have coverage in towns but gaps on open trail sections.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 112

Spain uses 112 as the EU emergency number. Specialist services: 091 (national police), 092 (local police), 061 (medical). All work without a plan.

🚌

Renfe and local transit apps

Spanish intercity rail books through Renfe — the app offers significant advance-booking discounts. Your eSIM data handles real-time platform information and the increasingly popular Bizum mobile payment system.

📱

Major sites require advance booking

The Prado, Sagrada Familia, and Alhambra require pre-booked timed tickets — the Alhambra especially sells out weeks in advance. Your eSIM data lets you check availability and manage bookings from anywhere.
