// Network operators

Guadeloupe Networks & Coverage

Orange Caraïbe

Widely considered the strongest overall network, especially around Deshaies and the mountainous, forested interior of Basse-Terre. The best pick if you are hiking or exploring beyond the main towns.

Digicel

Reliable across towns and the coast, including Pointe-à-Pitre, Le Gosier, and Sainte-Anne. Most international travel eSIMs use Digicel here. A third operator, SFR Caraïbe, also covers the island but less consistently than these two.

// Coverage by area

Coverage Quality in Guadeloupe

Pointe-à-Pitre, Le Gosier & Sainte-Anne

4G coverage is strong and consistent across the main towns and Grande-Terre's beach strip. Signal was reported solid throughout Sainte-Anne right down to the beach itself.

Deshaies & the Basse-Terre rainforest

Once you head into the hills around Deshaies or the volcanic, rainforest-covered interior of Basse-Terre, signal becomes noticeably less consistent, especially on Digicel and SFR. Orange tends to hold up best in these areas.

💡 Good news: the day-trip islands are covered too

Les Saintes, Marie-Galante, and La Désirade — popular day-trip destinations from the mainland — all get reasonable 4G/LTE from the premium networks (Orange and Digicel especially). Unlike some Caribbean destinations, a boat trip to these smaller islands does not mean going offline.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Your eSIM stops working at the border

A Guadeloupe or Martinique eSIM is tied to French/EU territory only. If you take the ferry to Dominica or Saint Lucia — both independent, non-EU countries — your connection will drop, and your home carrier may charge high roaming rates. Get a regional Caribbean eSIM if island-hopping beyond French territory.

The full French system, including 112

Guadeloupe uses 15 for SAMU (medical), 17 for police, and 18 for fire — standard across France. Unlike some Caribbean territories, the EU-standard 112 genuinely works here too, since Guadeloupe is real EU territory.

Physical SIMs need a passport

Buying a physical Orange, Digicel, or SFR SIM at Pointe-à-Pitre airport or a retail store means bringing your passport for ID. eSIM tourist plans skip this entirely — install before you fly and land already connected.
