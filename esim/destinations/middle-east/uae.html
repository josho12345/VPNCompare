// Network operators

UAE Networks & Coverage

📡

e& (formerly Etisalat)

The UAE's original national operator, now rebranded as e&. Widest coverage across all seven emirates including remote desert areas. 5G live in Dubai and Abu Dhabi. eSIM supported on compatible devices. Generally considered the stronger network for coverage depth.

📢

du (EITC)

The UAE's second operator, launched 2007. Strong urban coverage in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Sharjah. 5G expanding rapidly. eSIM supported. du tends to be competitive on pricing for tourist and short-term data plans. Good choice for city-focused travel.

// VPN & internet regulations

VPN Rules in the UAE

⚖

What is legal

VPN use by licensed businesses for legitimate corporate purposes is generally permitted. Companies operating in UAE free zones often use VPNs routinely for remote working and secure data transfer without issue.

🚫

What is not legal

Using a VPN to access content blocked in the UAE — including VoIP calls via WhatsApp, Skype, and FaceTime — is an offence under Federal Decree-Law No. 5 of 2012. Fines can reach hundreds of thousands of AED. This is enforced.

// What is blocked in the UAE

Blocked Apps & Services

📞

VoIP calls

WhatsApp calls, FaceTime, Skype, and most third-party VoIP services are blocked for personal use in the UAE. This is regulated by the TRA to protect revenue from licensed telecom operators e& and du. Text messaging on WhatsApp works fine. Video calls do not.

🌐

Other restrictions

Content considered contrary to Islamic values, politically sensitive material, and certain gambling and adult sites are blocked. Most mainstream apps and social media work normally. Netflix, Spotify, YouTube, Instagram, and major streaming services all operate in the UAE without restrictions.

📱

Making calls from UAE

Use regular international calls via your eSIM data plan for voice. Alternatively, both e& and du offer international calling packages for tourists that are reasonable for short stays. Check current rates at each operator's website before travelling.

Stay Secure in the UAE

👤

Protect your identity abroad

Hotel WiFi, airport hotspots, and mall networks in the UAE are as risky as anywhere else in the world. Avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on public networks. Use your eSIM data instead of hotel WiFi where possible.
