// Network operators

Malaysia Networks & Coverage

📡

CelcomDigi

Formed from the merger of Celcom and Digi, now Malaysia's largest carrier by subscriber count. Strong coverage across Peninsula Malaysia and the best option for Borneo (Sabah and Sarawak). 4G widely deployed. eSIM supported on tourist plans.

📢

Maxis

Malaysia's premium carrier. Excellent coverage in KL, Penang, and all major Peninsula cities. 5G expanding across urban areas. eSIM supported. The best choice for travellers spending most of their time in the Klang Valley or Penang. Slightly less strong than CelcomDigi in East Malaysia.

// The honest picture

Peninsula Malaysia vs Borneo

🏛

Peninsula Malaysia

KL, Penang, Langkawi, Melaka, the Cameron Highlands, and the East Coast beaches all have reliable 4G and growing 5G coverage. The North-South highway corridor is extremely well covered. Urban areas rival coverage quality in Western Europe.

🌳

Borneo (Sabah and Sarawak)

Coverage in Kota Kinabalu, Kuching, and main towns is fine. But rainforest, jungle trekking areas, and national parks can have very limited or no signal. Danum Valley, Maliau Basin, and remote river routes in Sarawak should all be treated as offline destinations. Download everything before you go.

💡 East Malaysia travel tip

If you are visiting Mount Kinabalu National Park for the summit climb, download your permits, guide contact numbers, and offline maps before leaving Kota Kinabalu. The park itself has limited connectivity and the summit approaches have none. This applies to most national park interiors in Sabah and Sarawak.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 999

Malaysia uses 999 as the unified emergency number (police, fire, ambulance). English-speaking operators are available. Tourist police desks exist at Kuala Lumpur International Airport and in central KL. Response quality is generally high in urban areas.

🚌

Grab is the standard here

Grab dominates ride-hailing and food delivery across Malaysia. Having active data from the moment you clear customs means you can book a Grab from KLIA or Penang International without queuing for a taxi. Your eSIM makes this instant.

📱

Touch 'n Go for everyday payments

Malaysia's Touch 'n Go e-wallet is used for highway tolls, transit, and a growing number of retail payments. Foreign visitors can use Touch 'n Go with an overseas card. Your eSIM keeps you connected for QR code payments across the country.
