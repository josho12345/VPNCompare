Why VPN Prices Vary So Much

VPN providers deliberately price monthly plans high — often $10 to $13 a month — to push users toward longer billing commitments. A 2-year plan is far more profitable for the provider since it locks in revenue and reduces churn, which is why the same VPN can cost $12 a month billed monthly or under $3 a month billed every two years. The headline "$2.49/mo" price you see advertised almost always assumes the longest available commitment.

This matters when comparing cheap VPNs, because the real question is not which provider has the lowest advertised price, but which provider delivers a genuinely audited, reliable service at that low price — rather than cutting corners on privacy or performance to hit an aggressive number.

What You Risk With an Unverified Cheap VPN

A low price by itself tells you nothing about safety. The VPNs worth recommending at a low price point share one thing in common: an independent, third-party audit of their no-log claims. Without that audit, a cheap VPN could be funding its low price by logging and selling user data — the opposite of what a VPN exists to do. Every VPN in this guide has a verified audit history, which is the minimum bar for inclusion regardless of price.

What to Check Before Choosing a Cheap VPN

Before committing to any low-priced VPN, confirm three things. First, has the no-log policy actually been independently audited, and by which firm. Second, what is the real billing structure — the advertised low price almost always requires a 2-year upfront commitment, so check the total amount charged rather than just the monthly figure. Third, what is the actual refund window if the service does not perform as expected once you start using it day to day.
