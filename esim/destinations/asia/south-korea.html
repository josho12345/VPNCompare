// Network operators

South Korea Networks & Coverage

📡

SK Telecom

South Korea's largest carrier. Exceptional 5G coverage across Seoul, Busan, Incheon, and all major urban areas. Consistently rated among the top three fastest mobile networks in the world. eSIM supported on international tourist plans. The benchmark carrier for Korea.

📢

KT (Korea Telecom)

South Korea's second carrier with equally strong 5G infrastructure. KT has excellent coverage including Jeju Island and secondary cities. eSIM supported. A reliable alternative to SK Telecom with comparable performance in all tourist areas travellers are likely to visit.

// What makes Korea different

Why Korean Networks Are So Fast

📱

Early 5G leadership

South Korea launched commercial 5G in 2019, one of the first countries to do so, and has spent heavily on dense urban 5G infrastructure since. Seoul metro stations, underground shopping areas, and even some subway cars have 5G signal. Coverage density rivals Japan and beats most European capitals.

🌈

Rural and island coverage

Jeju Island has reliable 4G/5G coverage across most of the island, including tourist areas and coastal routes. Rural areas of the peninsula generally have solid 4G. Genuine gaps are rare and mostly in mountainous national park areas where you would expect them.

💡 Check your device band compatibility

Korea's 5G relies primarily on n78 (Sub-6GHz / 3.5GHz). Most flagship phones from Samsung, Apple (iPhone 12 and later), and Google Pixel support this. Some budget Android phones and older iPhones may connect at 4G LTE instead — still fast, just not 5G. Worth checking your device specs before you pack.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 112 / 119

Police is 112, fire and medical is 119. Both have English-speaking operators available. Korea has an excellent emergency response infrastructure, and tourist areas like Myeongdong and Itaewon in Seoul have tourist police with English support.

🚌

T-money card for transit

Korea's T-money card handles subway, bus, and some convenience store payments. Your eSIM handles data and navigation while T-money handles transit fares — the two work together but are separate. Pick up a T-money card at any convenience store or subway kiosk on arrival.

🍲

Naver Maps beats Google Maps here

Google Maps has limited coverage data for South Korea due to mapping restrictions. Naver Maps or Kakao Maps give far better local navigation including transit directions, walking routes, and real business hours. Download them before you land.
