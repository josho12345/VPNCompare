// Network operators

Iceland Networks & Coverage

Síminn

Iceland's oldest and largest operator, and the clear benchmark for rural and remote coverage. Reaches deep into the Westfjords and the quieter stretches of the Ring Road where competitors thin out, with LTE coverage around 98% of the population. eSIM fully supported. The safest choice if your itinerary strays far from Reykjavík.

Nova

The budget favourite, and Iceland's first operator to launch 5G, back in 2020. Excellent value and strong performance around Reykjavík and the southwest, but LTE reach thins out considerably further from the capital. eSIM supported on tourist plans. Best suited to Golden Circle and South Coast itineraries rather than full Ring Road loops.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Iceland

Ring Road & main towns

Reykjavík, Akureyri, and every town along Route 1 have dependable 4G, with 5G expanding steadily. The Golden Circle, South Coast, and Snæfellsnes Peninsula, Iceland's most-visited regions, are all well covered.

The Highlands and F-roads

Iceland's volcanic interior, only reachable by F-roads and closed most of the year, has coverage described by Iceland's own road authority as intermittent at best. Do not rely on mobile data here; log your route with the Vegagerðin 112 app before you set off, regardless of signal.

🌋 The honest gap

Iceland's 9.0 score reflects genuinely strong coverage everywhere most visitors actually go: Reykjavík, the Ring Road, the Golden Circle, the South Coast. The real gap is specific and well known even to locals: the Highlands' F-roads, open only in summer and reachable only by 4x4, where coverage becomes unreliable regardless of network.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Emergency number: 112

Iceland uses a single number for every emergency: 112 covers police, fire, ambulance, and search and rescue alike, in English. It works even without an active SIM or credit, as long as there's signal, and can be reached by satellite (+354 809 0112) where there isn't.

Satellite phones are restricted for tourists

Unusually for a country this remote, Iceland restricts personal satellite phone use for visitors. The sanctioned alternative is the free 112 Iceland app: download it before your trip and use its GPS check-in feature whenever you head into the Highlands.

Uber doesn't operate here

Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft aren't available in Iceland. Taxis are safe and metered, but should be booked through an app like Hopp or Hreyfill rather than hailed. Cafes and public spaces commonly offer free WiFi — use your eSIM data for sensitive logins regardless.

// Travel security

Stay Secure in Iceland

Protect your connection on public WiFi

Icelandic café, hotel, and public WiFi networks carry standard risks, and Icelandic ISPs retain user metadata for six months by law. Use your eSIM data for banking and sensitive logins. A VPN on public WiFi is good practice and completely unrestricted.

Alternative ID for sign-ups

Icelandic booking platforms and tourist apps collect personal data. A disposable email and identity keeps your real information off databases you will not use again.
