// Network & tourist eSIM

Greenland's Network & Tourist eSIM

Tusass

Greenland's only mobile network, wholly state-owned and formerly known as TELE Greenland. Provides 4G LTE (no 5G yet) in the main coastal towns: Nuuk, Sisimiut, Ilulissat, Qaqortoq, Aasiaat, and Maniitsoq. Roughly 92% of Greenlanders live within its coverage — but that reflects population, not geography. The uninhabited majority of the country has none.

Hello Greenland

Tusass's own prepaid tourist product, built specifically for visitors: a local Greenlandic number, data, calls, and SMS, activated by QR code as either an eSIM or a physical SIM. No subscription, no recurring charges. Physical pickup is available at Tusass stores in Nuuk, Sisimiut, and Ilulissat if you'd rather not go digital.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Greenland

Coastal towns

Nuuk, Sisimiut, Ilulissat, Qaqortoq, Aasiaat, and Maniitsoq all have dependable 4G, and Kangerlussuaq's airport is covered too. Hotels and cafes in these towns commonly offer WiFi, which many travellers use to preserve data.

The ice sheet and fjord wilderness

Outside the coastal towns, there is no mobile coverage of any kind, not patchy, not occasional, none. The interior ice sheet, backcountry trekking routes, and boat crossings between settlements are all completely offline, regardless of season or which network you're on.

🌊 The honest gap

Greenland's 6.5 score isn't a narrow dip like most destinations on this site, it's a real structural split. Coverage is genuinely excellent in the handful of towns where the vast majority of the population lives, and genuinely absent everywhere else, which is most of the country by land area. If your trip stays in town, connectivity is a non-issue. If it doesn't, bring a satellite communicator, not just an eSIM.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Emergency number: 112

Unlike most of this site's destinations, Greenland doesn't split emergencies by service — 112 connects you to police, fire, and medical rescue alike, available in Kalaallisut, Danish, and English. It's free from any phone, with or without an active data plan.

There are no roads between towns

Greenland's settlements aren't connected by road — travel between them is by boat, helicopter, or small aircraft. Air Greenland runs the country's domestic network. Budget for offline stretches during any inter-town journey, however you make it.

Your eSIM doesn't work in the wilderness

Ice sheet crossings, ski touring, and backcountry hiking all fall outside any network's reach. A satellite communicator is standard safety equipment here — Garmin inReach, SPOT, or similar. An eSIM is not a substitute for one.

// Travel security

Stay Secure in Greenland

Protect your connection on public WiFi

Greenlandic café, hotel, and public WiFi networks carry standard risks, and are especially relied upon here given the cost of local data. Use your eSIM data for banking and sensitive logins. A VPN on public WiFi is good practice and completely unrestricted.

Alternative ID for sign-ups

Greenlandic and Danish booking platforms and tourist apps collect personal data. A disposable email and identity keeps your real information off databases you will not use again.
