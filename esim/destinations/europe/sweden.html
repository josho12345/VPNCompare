// Network operators

Sweden Networks & Coverage

📡

Telia Sweden

Sweden's largest carrier and the best choice for northern Sweden and Lapland. Excellent 4G/5G in Stockholm, Gothenburg, and all major cities. The only reliable option for travellers heading north of the Arctic Circle. eSIM supported.

📢

Tre (3 Sweden)

Strong competitor with excellent coverage in Stockholm, Gothenburg, and southern Sweden. Competitive 5G deployment in city centres. eSIM supported. Coverage drops significantly in northern Sweden — for anything above Sundsvall, Telia is the clear choice.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Sweden

🏛

Southern Sweden and major cities

Stockholm, Gothenburg, Malmö, Uppsala, Linköping, and all of southern Sweden have excellent 4G/5G coverage. The main rail routes between cities are well covered. Gotland island has reasonable coverage in Visby and main towns.

🏔

Northern Sweden and Lapland

Above the Arctic Circle — Abisko, Kiruna, and the E10 road to Narvik — coverage drops significantly. You will have signal in towns but open wilderness and the Swedish mountains should be treated as offline. The Inlandsbanan rail route has limited coverage on many stretches.

💡 Northern Lights travel and remote hiking

Northern Lights trips to Abisko and hiking in Sarek National Park should all be planned with offline-first thinking. Download offline maps, save your accommodation coordinates, and carry emergency information before leaving any northern town.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Emergency number: 112

Sweden uses 112 as the EU emergency number. Works without a plan. English-speaking operators are universal — Sweden has very high English proficiency. For mountain rescue in Lapland, call 112 and request Fjällräddning.

🚌

SJ rail and regional transport

Swedish intercity rail (SJ) connects Stockholm with Gothenburg, Malmö, and Uppsala efficiently. The scenic Inlandsbanan through northern Sweden has limited connectivity for long stretches. Book SJ tickets through the SJ app.

❄

Winter travel planning

If visiting Sweden in winter, download weather and road condition apps before you go. Swedish road conditions via Trafikverket are available by app. Rental cars often require winter tyres — confirm this when booking.
