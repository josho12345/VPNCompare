// Network operators

Bahamas Networks & Coverage

BTC

The Bahamas Telecommunications Company has the broadest national coverage, reaching New Providence, Grand Bahama, and many of the more visited Out Islands like Abaco and Exuma. The default backbone for most travel eSIM providers.

Aliv

The newer of the two carriers, with strong urban coverage in Nassau and Freeport and competitive pricing. A solid choice if your trip is staying close to the two main islands rather than island-hopping further out.

// Coverage by area

Coverage Quality in the Bahamas

Nassau, Paradise Island & Freeport

The two most-visited islands have strong, consistent 4G, with 5G beginning to appear in select Nassau neighbourhoods. New Providence and Grand Bahama between them cover the vast majority of visitor nights in the country.

The Out Islands

Beyond New Providence and Grand Bahama, coverage can disappear entirely. Popular spots like Exuma and Abaco have reasonable signal in settled areas, but many of the 700-plus islands and cays have no towers at all. Plan accordingly if you are island-hopping.

💡 Why coverage varies so dramatically

The Bahamas is not one island but an archipelago of over 700 islands and cays, only a fraction of which are inhabited. BTC and Aliv concentrate towers where the population actually is — New Providence and Grand Bahama overwhelmingly. Many Out Islands have limited or no emergency medical services either, reachable only by boat or helicopter, so it is worth researching connectivity and emergency access together before island-hopping.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Your home carrier might already include it

As the world's busiest cruise destination, the Bahamas is often bundled into US carriers' free international roaming passes at basic speeds. Worth checking before you buy an eSIM — though for anything beyond maps and messaging, a local eSIM will still feel noticeably faster.

Two numbers, same result

Both 911 and 919 connect to police, fire, and ambulance anywhere in the Bahamas. 919 is the traditional number; 911 was added given how many North American visitors call. On the more remote Out Islands, some have no emergency medical services at all, reachable only by boat or helicopter.

Physical SIMs need a passport

Buying a physical BTC or Aliv SIM at a retail store means bringing your passport for ID. eSIM tourist plans skip this entirely — install before you fly and there is no counter to queue at on arrival.
