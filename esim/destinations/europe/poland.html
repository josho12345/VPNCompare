// Network operators

Poland Networks & Coverage

Orange

Poland's largest operator by subscriber count, and the clear benchmark for rural and small-town reach, with LTE covering roughly 99.85% of the population. eSIM fully supported, with the most consistent English-language support of the four networks. The safest choice for travellers heading beyond the major cities.

Play

Poland's most-subscribed network, known for aggressive 5G expansion and consistently topping independent speed tests. Strong coverage in Warsaw, Kraków, and Wrocław. eSIM supported on tourist plans. A fast, budget-friendly alternative to Orange for city-focused itineraries.

// What to expect

Coverage Across Poland

Cities, towns, and major roads

Warsaw, Kraków, Wrocław, Poznań, and Gdańsk all have outstanding 4G and expanding 5G coverage, with 5G now live across every major city. Motorways and main rail corridors between towns are well covered too.

Rural eastern Poland

Smaller villages near the Belarusian and Ukrainian borders, and dense forest regions like Białowieża, can have weaker or inconsistent signal, even as coverage continues to shrink these gaps year over year. Orange and Plus generally perform best here.

💡 The honest rural gap

Poland's 8.8 score reflects genuinely excellent performance everywhere most tourists actually go: Warsaw, Kraków, Wrocław, and every major road between them. The real gap is narrower than it looks: it is specifically rural villages in the east, near the Belarusian and Ukrainian borders, not rural Poland generally, where signal becomes less consistent.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Emergency number: 112

Poland uses the EU standard: 112 covers police, fire, and ambulance alike, free from any phone, including those without an active SIM or credit. Operators speak English at the main dispatch centres.

The rail network is genuinely excellent

PKP Intercity connects every major city at high speed, and the Koleo and PKP Intercity apps handle tickets and live platform info. In Warsaw, the jakdojade app covers the metro, trams, and buses — your eSIM data runs both smoothly.

Uber, Bolt, and widespread WiFi

Both Uber and Bolt operate widely across Poland's major cities. Cafes and public spaces commonly offer free WiFi — but always use your eSIM data for sensitive logins rather than public networks.

// Travel security

Stay Secure in Poland

Protect your connection on public WiFi

Polish café, hotel, and public WiFi networks carry standard risks. Use your eSIM data for banking and sensitive logins. A VPN on public WiFi is good practice and completely unrestricted.

Alternative ID for sign-ups

Polish booking platforms and tourist apps collect personal data, and prepaid SIM purchases already require your passport under Poland's registration law. A disposable email and identity keeps your real information off databases you will not use again.
