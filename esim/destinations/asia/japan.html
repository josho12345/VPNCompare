// Network operators

Japan Networks & Coverage

NTT Docomo

Japan's largest and oldest network. Exceptional coverage everywhere, including mountains, rural towns, and hiking trails. 5G live across major cities. eSIM supported. The benchmark other networks are measured against.

SoftBank

One of Japan's three major carriers. Strong coverage in cities and along major transit routes, including the Shinkansen bullet train network. 5G expanding steadily. eSIM supported. A reliable, competitively priced choice for most travellers.

// Why it just works

Coverage Quality in Japan

Bullet trains and tunnels

The Shinkansen network has onboard signal boosters and tunnel repeaters installed along nearly the entire route, so you stay connected even travelling at 300km/h through mountain tunnels. Genuinely rare infrastructure by global standards.

Mountains and hiking trails

Even popular hiking routes like Mount Fuji's trails and much of the Japanese Alps have surprisingly reliable coverage, a rarity for mountainous terrain anywhere in the world. Do not expect this in every remote area, but it is far better than most countries manage.

💡 Why it works so well

Japan's carriers have invested heavily in dense tower infrastructure for decades, partly driven by domestic demand for reliable mobile internet long before it was common elsewhere. The result is a network most countries simply cannot match. Still, remote islands and deep countryside can have weaker spots — worth checking specific routes if you are heading somewhere very rural.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

Nothing is blocked

WhatsApp, FaceTime, Skype, Netflix, and every major streaming and communication app work exactly as normal. Japan has no internet censorship affecting everyday travellers. If it works at home, it works here.

Two emergency numbers

Japan uses 110 for police and 119 for fire or ambulance — not a single number like many countries. Both work without an active data plan. Worth saving both before you land, since the split trips up a lot of visitors.

IC cards still matter

Suica and Pasmo transit cards are separate from your eSIM and worth setting up on your phone's wallet app for trains and convenience store purchases. Your eSIM handles data, not transit fares — the two are not the same thing, and it trips up a lot of first-time visitors.
