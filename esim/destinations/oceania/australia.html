// Network operators

Australia Networks & Coverage

📡

Telstra

Australia's largest network by far. Reaches roughly 99% of the population and has by far the best regional and remote coverage of the three. 5G live in every major city. eSIM supported. Usually the most expensive of the three — you are paying for reach.

📢

Optus

Australia's second largest network. Strong coverage in cities and major regional towns, noticeably weaker than Telstra once you get remote. 5G expanding across metro areas. eSIM supported. Good balance of price and coverage for a city-and-coast trip.

// The honest gap

Outback & Remote Coverage

⚖

Cities and the coast

Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide, and the entire east coast tourist route are excellent for coverage. 5G in every metro area, strong 4G along all major highways connecting them. You will not notice any difference from a major European or US city.

🚫

The Outback and remote areas

Once you leave major towns, coverage drops off fast and can disappear entirely for hours at a time. Australia is enormous and much of the interior has no mobile infrastructure at all. This applies to every network, not just eSIM providers.

⚠ Our honest advice

No eSIM, however good, fixes a genuine coverage gap. If you are driving through the Outback, the Nullarbor, or any remote National Park, download offline maps, tell someone your route, and consider a satellite messenger for genuine emergencies. This is true regardless of which provider you choose — it is physics, not a sales pitch.

// Practical tips

Good to Know Before You Land

📞

Nothing is blocked

WhatsApp, FaceTime, Skype, Netflix, and every major streaming and communication app work exactly as normal. Australia has no internet censorship affecting everyday travellers. If it works at home, it works here.

🌐

Emergency number is 000

Triple Zero (000) works even without an active data plan or signal from your own carrier in many cases, as Australian law requires phones to attempt emergency calls on any available network. Worth knowing if you are ever in a genuine emergency in a low-coverage area.

📱

Time zones are real here

Australia spans three time zones and some states observe daylight saving while others do not. Your eSIM will show the correct local time automatically, which is more than can be said for half the group chats you will be coordinating with back home.
